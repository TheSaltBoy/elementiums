About

Gallium is a soft silver metal with a melting point so low it will melt in your hand.

Gallium isn't found very often in nature, and it's usually mixed with other elements.

Uses

Gallium is used in thermometers instead of Mercury since it's non-toxic and can withstand higher temperatures.

Gallium is more often used in electronics and circuitry. It's used to make blue and violet LEDs and lasers. It's also sometimes used in jewelry.

History

Dmitri Mendeleev, the creator of the Periodic Table, was the first preson to predict that Gallium existed. He originally named it Eka-Aluminium.

In 1875 French chemist Paul Emile Lecoq de Boisbaudran officially discovered Gallium using a Spectroscope, and he was the one to give the element its name.
