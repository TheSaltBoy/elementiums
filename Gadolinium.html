Gadolinium

Gaolinium is rare radioactive metal most often found in nuclear reactors.

About

Gadolinium is a silver metal. It's very ductile and malleable, meaning you can easily bend and stretch it. It's named after Johan Gadolin and Gadolinite, a mineral in which Gadolinium can be found. Naturally occuring Gadolinium is toxic to mammals.

Uses

When Gadolinium is combined with other metals it can help prevent them from oxidization, or rusting. It also absorbs neutrons and so is used as shielding in nuclear reactors.

History

Gadolinium is found in Gadolinite, which was named after Finnish chemist Johan Gadolin. In 1880 Swiss chemist Jean Charles Galissard de Marignac observed trace amounts of Gadolonium in Gadolinite and Cerite. He was later able to separate Gadolinium Oxide from the Cerite. He named it Gadolinia. Eventually, in 1886 another chemist named Paul Emile Lecoq de Boisbaudran separated Gadolinium metal from Gadolinia.
